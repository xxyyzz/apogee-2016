About Us

We have a population of around 1.2 billion and it is just a matter of time, that we will be the nation with the most number of young and technically sound work forces. Technology has grown in many forms and can be made accessible almost to all. It is just a luxury for some but can ease the life of many and can be the key to solving some of the most pressing issues faced by the country today. We just need some ideas and tinkering to apply it on the field.

An example, detecting and diagnosing diseases is a very time-consuming and expensive affair, almost unavailable in remote areas. An interesting fact - Today, smartphones have a better reach than basic healthcare facilities! Last year a student team developed a low-cost smartphone operated microscope, to detect malaria, though not accurately but the results were convincing enough to improve upon and take it further.

It is only by solving such basic problems that development will occur collectively in every community and not selectively. Dhiti(sanskrit for “An idea”) is a platform for passionate individuals who ideate and aspire for technology to reach the grassroots. Here we will provide you with problems and mentors to form feasible solutions to various issues and provide recognition to your solution. So, let’s put our ideas and knowledge bases to some real use.

Idea Proposal

Registrations for Round-1 are now Open. Submit an idea proposal (i.e., an Idea) before 10th of January, 2016 and earn yourself a chance to present your idea in Apogee.


Teams of maximum 4 members can participate in Dhiti. A pool of problem statements from specific areas have been uploaded. The problems are procured selectively from the suggestions of various NGOs working on the field.


Participants can either


Take up problem statements from those given under different specific areas and suggest technically and financially sound solutions. (From Given Problem Pool)


OR


Work on a problem identified by them in any given specific area and present a technically and financially feasible solution. (Open Category)

Mentorship

The Mentorship phase will kick off as soon as the results of the Idea Proposal phase are declared. Shortlisted teams will be given an option of mentorship.


We consider mentorship essential for the participants so as to give them an insight of the exact problems that are faced at the grassroots, of the situations and the constraints that may be involved during the implementation of their solutions. Mentorship will be provided in the following two forms:

  1. Technical assistance to the participants (without changing the basic idea of the solution), if required.
  2. Assistance in getting a clearer idea regarding the problem statement which will help the participants to arrive at a more suitable and feasible solution to the problem.

Assistance will be provided by a panel of experts having sound knowledge and experience. The allotment of mentors will be based on the problem and appropriateness of the solution.


Evaluation during Mentorship phase:


  1. The teams will be evaluated continuously during the mentorship phase.
  2. The participants proceeding to the final round will be decided based on the final report to be submitted after this phase, and feedback from the mentors. The decision of the judges will be final and binding.

Mentorship Phase ends on 6th of February, 2016. The teams are required to submit the final reports along with a video demonstration by 7th of February, 2016.


After this round, selected teams for the final round which will be conducted during the technical festival APOGEE 2016.

Implementation

The last phase will involve implementation of the solutions. Some solutions (from those selected for the final round) will be moved to the grassroots level, with help from the concerned NGOs in as many ways as possible. This might take the form of an internship based on the preferences of the organization and the students.

Presentation

  1. Selected teams will have to submit a hardcopy of their report, and give a final presentation on their solutions to a panel of judges, in APOGEE 2016, here at BITS PILANI, PIlani Campus.
  2. The teams would be provided a maximum time of 10 minutes for their respective presentations followed by a 5 minutes round of cross-questioning by our Judging panel.
  3. The presentation should cover all the details of the solution, namely, the idea, the methodology, cost-effectiveness, process of implementation, total cost which would be incurred for implementing the solution, its feasibility and sustainability.
  4. Along with the report and presentation, the finalists may come up with a working model of their solution, which explains their concept better, and hence, enhances clarity. (The best use you make of this, the more credit you shall be awarded).
  5. The Jury’s decision shall be deemed final and binding.
  6. Socially acceptable and environmentally benign.

Rules and guidelines

General Guidelines


  1. The competition is open to all (students, scholars, and passionate individuals).
  2. Teams of maximum 4 can participate in the event. The participants can be from different colleges.
  3. All participants are required to register on the Apogee 2016 website www.bits-apogee.org , and register for DHITI.
  4. The teams must submit their abstracts by 17th of January, 2016.
  5. Decision of the judges shall be treated as final and binding on all.
  6. At any point of time the latest information will be that which is on the website.However, registered participants will be informed through mail about any changes on the website.
  7. The ideas presented by the teams should be original. Plagiarism of any kind will lead to disqualification of the team.
  8. Certification – Shortlisted teams that present their solution in the final round in Apogee 2016 will receive a Participation certificate. The best solutions will be given appropriate certificates of distinction along with prizes and internships.

Judging Criteria


A few points to take note of while designing solutions -


  1. Unique and innovative: They can be a modification of an already existing solution but should not be an exact replica of it.
  2. Effective: They must provide better results than solutions which are already in use.
  3. Cost-effective: The materials being used for implementing should be easily available and cheap.
  4. Feasible: Keep in mind the target population/region. The ease of installation and the easy availability of the resources is also to be considered.
  5. Sustainable: The solutions should be designed in such a way that they can be easily modified in future to adapt to the needs and conditions at that time.
  6. Social Impact: Should have a large social impact. It should affect the living conditions of a large number of people rather than only a few.
  7. Replicable: Easily replicable so that they can be implemented over different regions rather than addressing a problem in a single particular area.

FAQs

1

Who can participate?

Any student, irrespective of his/her course or stream, who has registered for Apogee 2016 can participate in the event.

2

How do I register for Dhiti?

Registrations for Dhiti have started. The participant can register by filling the form at the Register button on top. He/she will also have to register for Apogee 2016 (visit http://www.bits-apogee.org ).

3

I am not an engineering student, can I still participate in the event?

Yes. Dhiti is open to students of all disciplines, pursuing any degree. Graduates, Post-graduates, Diploma students, PhD holders etc.

4

I am already working on a project that has the potential to reach the desired crowd. But my project is not a solution to any of the mentioned problem statements. Can I still participate?

Sure. As already mentioned in the structure, participants are encouraged to work on problem statements identified by them. This will come under the Open Category.

5

I have already presented this solution in another competition/ research paper, can I still present it here?

Yes. As long as there is no compulsion or binding on part of the participant from the concerned competition, the solution may be presented for Dhiti.

6

Is taking part in Implementation phase (the last phase) necessary?

It is not compulsory for the winners (or any other participant) to participate in the implementation (i.e. go to some place and get the idea implemented by yourself). The NGOs that are willing to implement the idea will do it themselves if you can't participate. But of course, your participation is encouraged and recommended. This might take the form of an internship based on the preferences of the organization and the students.

7

I am unclear about the Mentorship phase. What am I supposed to do during mentorship?

Mentorship will be given to the participants who clear the initial Idea Proposal phase. Mentorship will be given in two forms: During the mentorship phase, the selected participants are encouraged to contact the concerned NGOs for in-depth knowledge about the problems faced by them. Similarly, for any technical guidance, they may contact their mentor. Participants can also contact through mails or direct visits to the NGOs. The contacts will be provided after the participants clear Phase I. Necessary support from the Dhiti team will always be available.

8

Do I need to include the way of implementation of the solution in my report?

Not exactly, this platform requires you to just give a feasible technical solution to the problem. Its way of implementation will be finalized with the help of the NGO's in the Implementation phase, with the necessary pilot runs.

9

Is there any registration fee for the event?

No. The registration fees will be charged for Apogee only. There will not be separate registration fee for Dhiti.
