Internet of Things
“Over time the IoT is going to be very much like this fabled elephant — that it will be much, much bigger than any of us can imagine it being today.”
- CEO, Silicon Labs


The Internet of Things (IoT) is an environment in which objects, animals or people are provided with unique identifiers and the ability to transfer data over a network without requiring human-to-human or human-to-computer interaction. A thing, in the Internet of Things, can be a person with a heart monitor implant, a farm animal with a biochip transponder, an automobile that has built-in sensors to alert the driver when tire pressure is low -- or any other natural or man-made object that can be assigned an IP address and provided with the ability to transfer data over a network.

IoT has touched and changed human lives in myriad ways and is continuing to do so making machines smart human extensions.

IoT Challenge

IoT Challenge is a one-of-a-kind platform where participants will get all the required tools from Texas Instruments to give a real form to their creative IoT solutions.

The uniqueness and beauty of this challenge is that there will not be a particular problem statement. The teams can ideate an IoT solution in any of the facets of human life to make it simpler, easier, more convenient, or more systematic. The teams can explore their minds in a wide range of fields.

In the preliminary round, teams will have to submit their solution in a detailed written form on the online portal. The teams whose solutions are innovative and feasible will be selected for the final round. They will be provided a toolkit with all the necessary equipments to build a working prototype of their idea over a period of 25 days. The selected teams will present their prototypes at APOGEE-2016 before a panel of expert judges.

Each member of the winning team will be given TI’s smartwatch. So along with creating your own IoT prototype you can get one created by the maestros! All the teams presenting their solutions in the final round will get certificates from TI and their own to-be IoT device.

Rules
  • Participants have to form teams of 2-4.
  • In the preliminary round, teams will have to register by submitting their solution in a detailed written form on the online portal by 28nd Jan.
  • The teams with innovative and feasible solutions will be selected for the final round. Results of the preliminary round will be announced online.
  • The selected teams will be provided a tool kit by TI containing all the necessary equipments to build an IoT working prototype till 2nd Feb.
  • Use of non-TI processors will lead to disqualification.
  • The teamswill present their working prototype at APOGEE-2016 before a panel of expert judges.
  • Each member of the winning team will get TI's smartwatch. Apart from this, all the teams presenting their solution in the final round will get certificates from TI.

FAQs

1. Can teams be formed from different colleges or different years?

Yes

2. Is registration to be done before abstract submission? 

No. Registration will be done along with abstract submission.

3. In what form is the abstract to be submitted?

The abstract should contain a detailed description on reason of choosing the idea, the need of the device and the step by step execution plan to build it. It may include designs, structures, tables, etc. It will be a PDF file to be submitted on the online portal of APOGEE.

4. How will outstation students receive the tool kits?

The tool kits will be shipped to their respective places by the given date. They will have to make their own arrangements to reach Pilani at the time of APOGEE.

5. Can tools outside the tool kit be used for making the prototype? 

The tool kit will contain a Launchpad. The Energia IDE software of TI can be downloaded freely from the website - www.energia.nu. Participants can order sensors and other related ICs free of cost from the TI e-store.(They will have to place the order using their college email address). Apart from this, use of non-TI processors will lead to disqualification.

6. What will be the criteria for judgement?

For the preliminary round, solutions will be checked based on feasibility, uniqueness and scope of the idea. For the final round, solutions will be judged on the basis of implementation of the proposed plan and innovative use of tools.
